Evidence for better policy

Nepal’s historic transition to federalism has surged the demand for more and better data. There is an increasing need to transform existing data into actionable insights and policy outcomes. To achieve this Nepalis – across all the sectors – must have access to and ability to use the available data. Bearing this in mind, the World Bank is working to propel the data sharing and using capacity of wider population, and in the end, optimize the value of data.



Capacity Building

Nepal Data Literacy Program

Designed to catalyze stronger data driven decision-making by government and non-government actors across the country. As part of the program, a 100-hour training of the trainers workshop was delivered in 2019.

Solveathon 2019

A collaborative platform for professionals from diverse backgrounds to work on data-driven projects to tackle development challenges

Training on Applied econometrics

The course equips practitioners with the tools and techniques of applied statistics that could be used while working with complex set of economic and social data.

Data Projects

Provincial SDG Dashboard

A joint product of the National Planning Commission, Central Bureau of Statistics and the World Bank, this portal indicates province level updates on the Sustainable Development Goals.

Geo Node

This web-based, open source software enables users to access, share, and visualize geospatial data. It aims to solve a set of common, overlapping problems shared by different organizations.

Data in News

Tracks data and statistics used in different news portals. Also showcases information on the dataset size, date range, keywords and processes used for different levels of the analysis.

Knowledge Projects

Measuring inequality of Access modeling

Descriptive analysis of accessibility to various services at national, provincial and municipal scales, and suggests research possibilities unlocked by a general purpose model.

Nepal Development Update

World Bank Nepal's biannual publication on key economic developments over the preceding months and topics of policy significance. The December 2019 update focused on "Envisioning a future data ecosystem in a federal Nepal"

Open Data for Business Report

Presents learnings from an exploratory exercise to assess the use of data in banking, education and media sectors in Nepal. The report intends to inform a long-term strategy to increase the business use of government data in the country.

DSET (Data Survey Evaluation Tool)

World Bank in partnership with the Open Data Watch evaluated 22 publicly available data portals in Nepal using its Data Site Evaluation Toolkit.
